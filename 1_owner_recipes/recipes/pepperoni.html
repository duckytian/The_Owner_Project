Pepperoni Pizza

A pizza topped with tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and slices of pepperoni—a spicy cured sausage made from pork and beef seasoned with paprika and chili peppers. It’s one of the most popular pizza varieties worldwide, known for its bold flavor and crispy texture when baked.

Ingredients:

Dough:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Toppings:

  • ½ cup pizza sauce (tomato sauce)
  • 1 ½ cups mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 20–30 pepperoni slice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for brushing the crust)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano or Italian seasoning (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon parmesan cheese (optional)

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour, salt, sugar, and yeast. Add warm water and olive oil. Mix until a soft dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  3. Place the dough in a bowl, cover it, and let it rise for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.
  4. Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F).
  5. Roll or stretch the dough into a 12-inch circle and place it on a pizza pan or baking tray.
  6. Spread the pizza sauce evenly over the dough, leaving about 1 inch around the edges.
  7. Sprinkle the mozzarella cheese evenly, then place the pepperoni slices on top.
  8. Sprinkle oregano and parmesan cheese if desired. Brush the crust lightly with olive oil.
  9. Bake for 12–15 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Remove from the oven, let it cool for a few minutes, slice, and serve.
